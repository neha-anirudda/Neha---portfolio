PRD-01 Skeleton-Based Yoga Motion Generation Research Project
+
Problem

Most motion generation models work well for fast, generic actions but struggle with slow, stability-heavy movements like yoga often producing jittery and biomechanically inconsistent sequences.

Research focus

Understanding whether generative models can learn structured, temporally consistent yoga motion not just replicate poses, but generate smooth and diverse transitions.

Key decisions
  • Used skeleton-based data (3D joints) instead of video to improve control and interpretability.
  • Adopted a CVAE + GRU architecture to balance temporal modeling with generative diversity.
  • Introduced motion-aware augmentation and stability anchoring to reduce jitter and improve early-frame consistency.
  • Benchmarked against Seq2Seq and standard VAE baselines to validate improvements.
Trade-offs
  • Diversity vs. stability — increasing variation can introduce slight anatomical inconsistencies.
  • Evaluation limits — metrics capture smoothness and realism, but not fully human-perceived quality.
Result: +59% improvement in generative diversity over Seq2Seq with improved realism (lower Motion-FID) and stable motion generation.

PRD-02 Zomato — Product Teardown Teardown
+
What works
  • Order-tracking is genuinely excellent — live map and contextual nudges reduce anxiety during the worst part of the wait.
  • Search-to-order friction is low if you already know what you want.
  • Gold membership reframes a discount as a status object, not just a coupon — smart retention lever.
What doesn't
  • Discovery is broken for indecisive users — the homepage throws 14 carousels at you and none help you decide.
  • Post-order support is a maze: 3-4 taps to a resolution, right when the user's patience is lowest.
  • Reviews are noisy and rarely tied to your actual order context.
Specific improvements I'd ship
  • A "Decide for me" mode — 2-3 taps (mood, budget, time) → one confident recommendation.
  • One-tap order-issue resolution surfaced directly on the tracking screen.
  • Review tagging by issue type (late / cold / wrong item / portion) so ratings become a diagnostic signal, not just a star count.
PRD-03 CopDrop – Product Breakdown Critique
+
What works

Clear discovery concept in seconds. Strong search. Feels like a homegrown brand-first platform.

Key gaps
  • Problem not explicit : value feels implied, not stated.
  • Homepage stays static after login → no personalization.
  • Messaging is product-led, not user-led.
Opportunities
  • Personalized feed: “You liked X → Try Y”
  • Intent-based search (style + budget)
  • Trend signals (“Trending this week”)
UX improvements
  • Guest session before signup
  • Save user behavior pre-login
  • Reduce whitespace, improve content density
Takeaway

Strong base. Next step: shift from static browsing → AI-driven personalized discovery.
